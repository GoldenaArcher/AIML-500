# AIML-501 Assignment 4: Explainable AI, Validation, and Trust in LLMs

## Explanatory Document

This graphic explains that trust in large language models depends on more than a model giving a confident answer or a polished explanation. The main points are that LLMs are hard to interpret because of their black-box structure, post-hoc explanations can be incomplete, and data quality still affects fairness and reliability. It also shows that explainability alone is not enough; validation, benchmark testing, human review, safety checks, and monitoring are what provide measurable evidence that a model is trustworthy and effective in real use.

I designed the page as an infographic with four visual blocks so readers can move through the topic in a practical order: meaning, challenges, validation, and current industry techniques. That structure keeps the content accessible for mixed audiences and makes the relevance clearer, especially for anyone trying to understand how explainability, validation, and performance metrics work together in real AI systems.
