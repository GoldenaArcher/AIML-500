# AIML-501 Assignment 2: Neural Network Components

I put this artifact together as a visual explanation of neural network components, but I also wanted it to reflect how I actually worked through the playground myself. I learn better from visual comparison, so taking screenshots from different runs made the differences much easier to understand than just writing definitions. That was especially helpful here because the assignment asked for both component explanations and visual presentation.

What made the playground more useful for me was seeing how the decision boundary changed when I adjusted layers, neurons, and activation functions. The smaller networks were clearly too weak for the circle dataset, while the deeper tanh and ReLU models fit the pattern much better. Trying sigmoid and linear activation also made the contrast more obvious, because both looked much weaker even when the network depth stayed similar.

Most of my revisions were about making the artifact easier to follow. I grouped the content into component definitions, experiment results, and a short summary so the page would not feel like a long technical dump. I also selected screenshots that showed both weak and strong results, since that comparison made the component explanations more meaningful.
